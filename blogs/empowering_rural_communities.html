Empowering Rural Communities: A Glimpse into the 90-Day Non-Residential Training on Elderly and Child Care

29 October, 2023

In a significant stride towards community welfare, the serene village of Bharko in Samvad Sutra, Amarpur (Banka) recently witnessed the inauguration of a transformative 90-day non-residential training camp. Panchayat head Diwakar Jha lit the ceremonial lamp to mark the beginning of this noble initiative, which focuses on the proper care of the elderly and children under the Prime Minister's Scheme.

Nestled near the picturesque Purna Pokhar in Bharko village, the Anganwadi center became the epicenter for this crucial training program. More than five dozen enthusiastic men and women, aged between 20 to 45 years and hailing from various villages within Bharko Panchayat, actively participated in the training sessions.

The Initiative's Leadership:

Guided by the Sevadeep Foundation, the initiative was spearheaded by a team committed to community well-being. The Managing Director of the Foundation, Robin Smith, emphasized the primary focus of the scheme – the mental development and healthcare of the elderly and children residing in rural areas.

The training program, a brainchild of the Prime Minister's Scheme, aims to bridge the gaps in healthcare and mental well-being within rural communities. Robin Smith highlighted the critical need for proper care, especially for the elderly, citing the potential risks of irritability and various serious diseases that may arise due to neglect. Additionally, the initiative addresses the absence of systematic care and health checkups for children.

Key Components of the Training:

The 90-day curriculum is meticulously designed to cover a spectrum of topics essential for the holistic development of both elderly individuals and children. Participants engage in interactive sessions focusing on mental health, physical well-being, and specialized care requirements tailored to the unique needs of these two vulnerable demographics.

The Role of Sevadeep Foundation:

The Sevadeep Foundation, known for its commitment to community service, played a pivotal role in orchestrating the training. Robin Smith expressed the Foundation's dedication to uplifting rural communities by fostering awareness and providing essential skills for compassionate elderly and child care.

The enthusiastic response from more than five dozen participants underlines the community's commitment to embracing positive change. As the training progresses, the impact is expected to resonate not only within individual households but also throughout the entire Bharko Panchayat.

The 90-day non-residential training on proper care for the elderly and children stands as a beacon of hope for rural communities. Through the tireless efforts of the Sevadeep Foundation and the active participation of Bharko villagers, this initiative holds the promise of transforming lives and nurturing a healthier, more compassionate society. As the lamp of knowledge continues to burn bright in the Anganwadi center, the glow of positive change is poised to radiate across the tranquil landscapes of Samvad Sutra.
